What is Live Ops?

Live Ops (Live Operations) is the team responsible for monitoring and managing live streams in real time. When a live event starts — whether it's a sports match, a gaming tournament, or a broadcast — Live Ops ensures the stream reaches viewers without interruption.

This involves watching bitrate graphs, responding to source failures, switching between backup sources, communicating through internal channels, and escalating issues before viewers notice anything went wrong.

🎯

Your responsibilities

  • Monitor bitrates — Watch the incoming bitrate from the source encoder. A healthy stream stays within expected bounds. Drops or flatlines need immediate attention.
  • Start, delay, or abandon — When a stream is due to start, you decide: start it on schedule, hold it if the source isn't ready, or abandon if things look unrecoverable.
  • Coordinate via Slack — Communicate with producers, engineers, and backup teams in real time using structured Slack messages.
  • Switch to backup sources — If the primary source fails, you switch to a pre-configured backup to keep the stream alive.
  • Log and escalate — Document what happened, when, and why. Flag anything unusual for the engineering team.
